Want to be a big boy and flex on your friends?

I use Gentoo. No, not because it’s easy—I compile my entire system from source, one USE flag at a time. Gentoo isn’t an operating system; it’s a rite of passage. It’s spending 12 hours building a toolchain just to boot into a terminal and think, “Yes. This is purity.” Why do I use Gentoo? Optimization. Fine-grained control. The zen-like calm that comes from watching GCC do its sacred work for hours, knowing that when something breaks, it’s because I dared to optimize too hard. Other distros give you binaries like a fast-food meal. Gentoo hands you raw ingredients and a knife, and says, “Cook, or starve.” And I cook. I feast. Kernel panic? Enlightenment. Dependency hell? A test of character. I don’t just run a system—I curate it. My CFLAGS are tuned tighter than your life goals. My init system isn’t just functional—it’s art. Oh, you update your system with apt? That’s adorable. I update mine with emerge -avuDN --with-bdeps=y @world, and then I rebuild everything because I changed one USE flag. Desktop environment? Please. I breathe OpenRC and dream in Makefiles. My rice setup isn’t just minimal—it’s spiritually aligned with Plan 9. People say Gentoo users are elitist. That’s not an insult. That’s the application form.
